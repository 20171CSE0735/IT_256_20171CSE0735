VIJAY

Vijay was born on 22 June 1974[1] as Joseph Vijay in Madras (now Chennai). His father S. A. Chandrasekhar is a Tamil film director and his mother Shoba is a playback singer and carnatic vocalist. He had a sister Vidhya, who died when she was two years old. His sisters death affected Vijay greatly; according to his mother, Vijay, who was very talkative, naughty and hyperactive as a child, became silent after Vidhya's death.[18] Vidhya's story is depicted in the 2005 film Sukran, in which Vijay played an extended cameo.[19][better source needed] He is a Roman Catholic, and received his first Holy Communion at a church in Chennai.[20] Initially attending Fathima Matriculation Higher Secondary School[21] at Kodambakkam, he later joined Balalok Matriculation Higher Secondary School[22] at Virugambakkam and went on to pursue a degree in Visual Communications from Loyola College. He eventually dropped out as he was more interested in acting.[18] Vijay married a fan,[23] Sangeetha Sornalingam,[24] a Hindu Sri Lankan Tamil whom he met in the United Kingdom, on 25 August 1999.[25] They have two children, a son Jason Sanjay born in 2000 in London[26] and a daughter Divya Shasha born in 2005 in Chennai.[27] Jason Sanjay made a cameo appearance with his father in Vettaikaaran (2009) and Divya Shasha portrayed a small role as her father's pre-teen daughter in Theri (2016).
